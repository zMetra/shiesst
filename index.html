Our History

The Shiesty Buhls have a rich and intriguing history that dates back to the early 20th century. It all started with the vision of one man, Frank Henry Buhl.

Frank Henry Buhl

Frank Henry Buhl was a prominent industrialist and philanthropist from Sharon, Pennsylvania. Born in 1869, he made a significant impact on the region through his various business ventures and community contributions.

Buhl's entrepreneurial spirit led him to establish several successful companies, including the Sharon Steel Corporation, which played a vital role in the growth of the steel industry in the area.

Moreover, Buhl was deeply committed to philanthropy and believed in giving back to the community. He funded numerous educational and cultural institutions, leaving a lasting legacy that continues to benefit the region to this day.

Buhl Mansion

A notable architectural gem associated with Frank Henry Buhl is the Buhl Mansion. Constructed in the early 1890s, this magnificent residence showcases the opulence and grandeur of the Gilded Age.

The Buhl Mansion, located in Sharon, Pennsylvania, is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship and luxurious features. It stands as a testament to the vision and refined taste of Frank Henry Buhl.

Today, the Buhl Mansion serves as a boutique bed and breakfast, offering guests a chance to experience the elegance and history of the Buhl family.

As the Shiesty Buhls embrace their shrewdness and cunning, they draw inspiration from the legacy of Frank Henry Buhl and the timeless elegance of Buhl Mansion.
